Technical Abstract (Limit 200 words) Foster-Miller and Cornell University propose to develop very small, low cost sensors for use in aircraft gas turbine engines at temperatures up to 600 oC using microfabrication of silicon nitride based mechanical devices. The results of Phase I will show the feasibility of a high temperature vibration sensor, including building and testing prototype sensors at temperatures from room temperature to 600oC. The data should verify that the new sensors will be useful for sensing and control systems on gas turbine test cells and in service applications for engine health monitoring and autonomous control. Phase II will involve placement of sensors in operating gas turbines, and will show feasibility sensors based on optical fiber interconnection. 8. Potential Commercial Application(s)(Limit 200 words) There is an urgent need for the sensors and control systems in commercial aircraft gas turbines, and in other turbine applications for shipboard power and electric power generation. The annual worldwide market for such sensors and control systems is estimated to be in the hundreds of millions of dollars. The high temperature microfabricated sensors will also find uses in automotive and truck engines, and for heavy industries including steel, chemicals, and other raw material processing.
