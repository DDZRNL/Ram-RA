Technical Abstract (Limit 200 words) Magnetic bearings represent an important candidate technology for aerospace applications. They offer improved efficiency (through complete elimination of friction) and reliability (from reduced wear) over conventional bearings. Commercial development, however, has been impeded largely by cost considerations, chiefly those accruing to rotor displacement sensors. Self- sensing technology, wherein rotor displacement is inferred through the information content of the amplifier current signals, is now a widely accepted paradigm that allows rotor position sensors to be eliminated. In past SBIR work for NASA, Barron Associates, Inc. developed a polynomial neural network self- sensing solution that covers the magnetic non- saturation and saturation regimes, the latter where the relationship between rotor displacement and bearing currents is highly nonlinear. The proposed Phase I STTR research will focus on extending this neural network approach to dynamic scenarios, in which rotor position and actuation currents are time-varying. Dynamic scenarios are important for practical usage of the technique. The Phase II sequel will bring this technology to fruition in the form of a laboratory spinning rig demonstration of the self-sensing technology operating in the saturation regime. 8. Potential Commercial Application(s)(Limit 200 words) Commercial applications of magnetic bearings include a host of aeronautic and niche industrial applications, such as aircraft engine and power systems, rotating machinery, flywheels, kinetic compressors, and heart pumps. Self- sensing will play a decisive role in making magnetic bearing technology cost effective and sufficiently reliable. Embedded polynomial neural network algorithms for self-sensing in integrated power units (IPUs) for aircraft power systems and in high-speed pumps for the petrochemical industry are the two commercial applications on which the STTR work will focus in collaboration with Sundstrand Aerospace, the Barron Associates, Inc. commercialization sponsor.
