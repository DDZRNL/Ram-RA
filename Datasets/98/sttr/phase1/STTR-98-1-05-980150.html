Technical Abstract (Limit 200 words) The objective of this work is to design new signal processing algorithms for a "smart" eddy current array sensor system that will enable gas turbine blade diagnostics and high cycle fatigue prognosis. The approach uses fast, adaptive wavelet transforms for spatio-temporal mechanical signature analysis to extract diagnostic information from measured signal patterns in real time. The methodology combines computational modeling of the sensor waveform based on physical principles of the sensor operation and data-driven analysis of blade model vibration and stress mode shapes. Preliminary experimental tests show that the sensor and wavelet analysis can detect variations in blade configuration, and can indicate the on-set of engine stall. The work will contribute to the implementation of new on-line high performance health monitoring for turbine engines. It will leverage ongoing development of eddy current sensors with high performance, efficient computational algorithms that support the deployment of sensor arrays. The formulation is general and can be adapted to other types of sensors and other diagnostic and prognostic health monitoring applications. 8. Potential Commercial Application(s)(Limit 200 words) The application potential for an on-board blade health monitoring is tremendous. This work will lead to new sensor technologies and signal processing algorithms for diagnostics and prognostics for high-performance turbine engine systems. It will contribute to new methods of health monitoring for these critical components. The director of Prognostics and Health Management for the Joint Strike Fighter at Pratt & Whitney has already indicated his interest in our preliminary findings. The signal processing technology will be marketed jointly with GD/ATS to prime contractors and engine manufacturers.
