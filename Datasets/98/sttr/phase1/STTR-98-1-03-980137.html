Technical Abstract (Limit 200 words) The proposed project will address two important issues in power quality and power density in non-linear AC/DC power systems. A new power switching technique known as soft-switching will be used to design high-power density systems with improved efficiency while achieving near unity power factor. Unlike the traditional passive approach, the newly developed technology by the University of Central Florida will result in total elimination of line current distortion, resulting in near unity power factor (PF) and low Total Harmonic Distortion (THD). This new soft-switching technique will result in more than 30% reduction in the size of the switching power supplies currently installed at KSC facilities. Different AC/DC power factor topologies will be compared with respect to their efficiency, and power density (size and weight). Also, the application of the new PFC topologies in the design of Distributed Power Systems (DPS) along with their cost and control complexity will be investigated. 8. Potential Commercial Application(s)(Limit 200 words) Users of computer systems and other control systems that use AC/DC power supplies stand to gain the most due to energy cost and system performance. Other potential applications include vehicle technology, space communication systems, phased-radar arrays and satellites. Furthermore, the proposed DPS control scheme is an essential system for the design of high performance, high power density systems used extensively in commercial and military applications.
