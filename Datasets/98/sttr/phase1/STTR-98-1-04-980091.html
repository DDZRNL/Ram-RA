Technical Abstract (Limit 200 words) A thermal method for directly applying LaRC-IA polyimide as a high-quality spray-foam as an insulation onto a substrate, such as fuel or cryogenic liquid tanks, will be developed and demonstrated. The proposed technology will lower the costs of using polyimide foams by providing a method of applying specialty, advanced lightweight, polyimide foams to directly onto complex shapes and structures. The project shall include the spraying of NASA's newly invented LaRC-IA polyimide shapes to form an adherent, mechanically-sound, insulating foam. The resulting advanced polyimide foams will be lighter and can be used for higher temperature applications than existing polymer sprayable foams. The novel thermal spray method will convey, heat and deposit the advanced LaRC- IA polyimide materials onto substrates. A key aspect of the Phase I work includes the development of polyimide bonding methods that are compatible with the novel thermal spray technology. Phase I will establish feasibility of the process and be used to evaluate three different polyimide material bonding methods. Phase II work will demonstrate the operation and control of a prototype process suitable for spraying polyimide foams onto a functioning substrate, such as a fuel or cryogenic liquid tank. 8. Potential Commercial Application(s)(Limit 200 words) The proposed project will promote the use of newly developed polyimide materials as high-temperature insulation on NASA, military and commerical spacecraft, such as the space shuttle, the X-33 and future Reusable Launch Vehicles (RLV). Applications for quothigh-performance" foams, such as the polyimide foams proposed herein, particularly sprayable foams, currently exist in aerospace, marine, aircraft, automotive, recreational and electronics markets.
