Technical Abstract (Limit 200 words) Significant research and development is underway for NGST and SIM regarding steady state wavefront error correction. However, little appears to have been developed in regard to dynamic wavefront error correction. Yet, the issue of minimizing dynamic error correction requirements by eliminating on-axis accelerations and off-axis accelerations is of crucial importance to NGST, SIM and other astrophysics missions. Introducing controlled isolation will minimize on-axis accelerations and off-axis accelerations (via electronic differential control) by integrating smart materials and advanced electronics design. This new concept is multifunctional since it serves to effectively isolate the space bus and the scientifc instrumentation module and simultaneously acts to implement pointing axis correction for the entire OTA assembly as to reduce the fine error correction requirements burden on the active optics. 8. Potential Commercial Application(s)(Limit 200 words) The results of this research are important for 'real world' military and commercial applications. EMF is exploring with TRW Civil & International Division, Harris Corporation Mililtary and Space Division and Boeing ISDS, the introduction of a practical compact, lightweight isolation system to a wide range of launch system and astrophysics platform applications. The technology has direct migration to automotive systems, machinery mounts and isolation tables.
