Technical Abstract (Limit 200 words) Development of affordable, high performance nuclear engines enable a quantum leap in the capability to carry out exploration and commercialization of the solar system, particularly with regard to the more distant planets and moons. Utilization of nuclear engines will result in much shorter trip times and much smaller and cheaper launch vehicles that contribute to dramatic reductions in mission costs. A new ultra-compact nuclear engine concept, MITEE (MIniature reacTor EnginE), is described, and its performance evaluated for various solar system exploration missions. The MITEE concept will serve as a temporary bridge to the development of very advanced and much higher Isp rockets based on nuclear fusion/antimatter which will be required for the exploration and colonization of the solar system. The company plans to apply and extend the knowledge gained from the MITEE project to the systems research and development of fusion/antimatter engines. Representative design parameters for the baseline MITEE reactor are: 75 MW(th) power level, 1000 seconds Isp, 100 kg mass, 10 MW/Liter fuel element power density, 39 cm core diameter/height. Total engine mass, including turbopump, nozzles, controls, and contingency is estimated to be 200 kg. Using the MITEE engine, ultra-fast, lightweight solar system exploration missions are enabled. 8. Potential Commercial Application(s)(Limit 200 words) Satellite and telecommunications manufacturers would have a vested interest in derivative applications of the MITEE engine. Compact heat engines based on this technology would provide electric power in the kilo and megawatt range for satellite operations. Major aerospace companies can benefit from space satellite retrieval/orbit changes from orbital transfer/maneuvering vehicles based on bimodal engine configurations which entail both propulsion and power producing capabilities. In addition, small powerplants can be constructed to provide energy for usage in remote and isolated outposts both on Earth and other celestial bodies. Energy conversion techniques can include static applications as a replacement for radioisotope thermoelectric generators (RTG), or dynamic applications as seen in heat engines utilizing the Brayton or Rankine cycles. In a novel concept, low cost, robust outposts can be established with indigenous resources processed to manufacture breathable air, food and stockpile propellants, fuel, and construction materials. Space mining can be made cost- effective with other MITEE technology derivatives even with the use of today's existing launchers. Another application of this technology centers on designing nuclear engines that enable low-cost distant intercept missions against Near Earth Object impactors that the Earth with catastrophic collisions.
