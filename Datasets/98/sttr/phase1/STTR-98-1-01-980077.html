Technical Abstract (Limit 200 words) Avyd Devices, Inc. and Rensselaer Polytechnic Institute propose an approach which combines the advantages of the capability to grow high crystalline quality epitaxial layers of GaN and GaAlN via the metalorganic chemical vapor deposition (MOCVD) technique with a p type doping approach which could result in much higher conductivity p type GaN and GaAlN layers and hence much improved p type contact resistance and quantum efficiency along with a much sharper UV spectral response facilitating efficient rejection of the longer wavelength radiation. Phase I emphasis will be focused on demonstrating the feasibility to develop a nitride based p-i-n UV detector technology capable of providing detectors with high responsivity, low noise and high speed. In phase I we plan to demonstrate and deliver to NASA single element and linear arrays of p-i-n photodetectors of GaN . In phase II, we plan to demonstrate and deliver 256x256 imaging arrays of these devices. 8.
