Technical Abstract (Limit 200 words) We propose to produce a compact stabilized tunable diode laser system emitting 630.5 nm light with frequency drift reduced to less than one part per billion over periods longer than 10 hours. A novel atomic-reference-free stabilization scheme developed by Dr. John Hall adapted for external cavity diode lasers engineered at Focused Research will provide absolute frequency calibration. Uniquely, this system will stabilize any wavelength selected within the laser's tuning range, enabling new scientific and industrial applications. The method is applicable to existing visible and infrared wavelengths and we can satisfy NASA's needs for ultrastable lasers at shorter wavelengths as new laser diodes become available. 8. Potential Commercial Application(s)(Limit 200 words) Commercial opportunities for economical, robust, and tunable wavelength- stabilized lasers include industry applications such as telecommunications test equipment, high-resolution interferometry, and micropositioning, surface defect inspection, and low-power LIDAR (laser radar). The proposed system would be especially well suited to a variety of spectroscopic applications in the scientific and engineering communities as well. Dr. Hall is renowned for pioneering high-performance laser frequency locking techniques. Focused Research and its parent company, New Focus, is a technology leader in tunable external cavity diode lasers ideally suited to frequency locking. Combining our expertise in laser design and stabilization methods, we will achieve the low cost and high performance necessary for the industrial stabilized laser market.
