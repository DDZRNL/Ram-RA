Technical Abstract (Limit 200 words) It is recognized that nuclear fusion is the only known source that can provide the prodigious amounts of power required for deep space missions. This is due to both the large and available quantities of fuel. Of all fusion reactor embodiments, only the magnetically confined plasma in the Field Reversed Configuration (FRC) has the ideal linear geometry, the low confining field and high plasma pressure required for the direct conversion of fusion energy into high specific impulse and thrust. The FRC has demonstrated in experiments the scaling with size and density required for fusion ignition. Due to the high plasma pressure and compactness of the magnetic configuration, there has been no easy way to extrapolate the FRC into the reactor regime. Based on experiment, a simple way has been determined by which the FRC can be readily brought to ignition conditions. This state is achieved by employing an Inductive, Magnetized Plasma Accelerator (IMPAC). It is the intention of MSNW to design (Phase I), and construct (Phase II) the prototype IMPAC source. The source alone, when externally powered, could produce the thrust and Isp necessary for a fast Mars mission in a device smaller than a desk. 8. Potential Commercial Application(s)(Limit 200 words) A successful outcome of the IMPAC based fusion rocket would of course, at the very least, allow man to explore and colonize the planets, and possibly extend man's reach to other worlds beyond our solar system. Certainly NASA would be a customer, as no doubt any other organization, governmental or not, that value this same vision for mankind. In the short term, the IMPAC source would provide a small, extremely efficient, propulsion device at very low cost, and development time. The IMPAC source would provide more than enough Isp and thrust to accomplish rapid space travel to nearby planets, and would make possible the commercial use of space beyond the earth. Perhaps though, the major spin-off of this power source, a terrestrially based fusion reactor, would have the most significant commercial application. The customer in this case would essentially be the entire world, as fusion represents the only long term, non-polluting, energy source that could supply the earth's energy needs.
