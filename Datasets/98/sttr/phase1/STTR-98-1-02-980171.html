Technical Abstract (Limit 200 words) Our program is based on these premises: (1) Candidate processes for production of individual products on Mars and the Moon, e.g., oxygen for life support and propulsion, silane for manufacture of silicon chips for solar-electric power generation and propulsion, and ceramics/metals for structures, helium 3 for electric power generation (fusion), hydrogen for propulsion, and water for life support, share common processes; (2) silicon and silicon carbide based microchannel and MEMS (Micro-Electro Mechanical Systems) technology can produce highly efficient components to conduct critical ISRU processes such as electrolysis of water to form hydrogen and oxygen and the reduction of carbon oxides to form useful products, such as propellants, structural materials, and foodstuffs; and (3) near term ISRU demonstration experiments will greatly benefit from ultra-lightweight, compact, integrated chemical processing systems which can be produced using microchannel technology. Specific Phase I program objectives are to design and fabricate silicon microchannel components and to perform bench scale demonstration of ISRU processes important for use on Mars and the Moon. Phase II will develop an engineering prototype system integrating components for quotend-to-end" production of valuable products, such as propellant, for sample return and manned Mars missions. 8. Potential Commercial Application(s)(Limit 200 words) MEMS and microchannel technology is an emerging field with applications in most industrial processes due to excellent mixing, controlled reaction environment, and energy efficiency. This technology offers improvements in existing processes and will enable new processes to become cost effective. Of particular interest is the petrochemical industry for which products from conversion of carbon oxides to stable, useful compounds such as methane and ethylene is of major importance.
