6. Technical Abstract (Limit 200 words)

We propose a real-time diagnosis, prognosis, and mission planning solution for complex systems. The proposed effort leverages QSI's real- time model-based diagnosis engine, TEAMS-RT, and UConn's expertise in the areas of signal processing, estimation and prognosis. The resultant solution will collect sensor data, process it, and assess system readiness in real-time. In the event of degradation or failure, the source will be isolated promptly, and its impact on mission evaluated. If the failure is in a redundant subsystem, the reconfigured system can continue with its mission, possibly in a degraded mode. Otherwise, the remaining life or extent of the failure will be estimated, and mission priorities and available capabilities matched to modify the mission or to select an alternative mission. A unique feature of the proposed effort is that the failure detection, isolation and reconfiguration modules can operate remotely over a low bandwidth wireless link. This can be utilized by ground support to assess system health and to shadow the onboard reasoner. More significantly, this tele-diagnosis capability will enable real-time health monitoring and on- demand diagnosis of legacy systems that lack onboard reasoners. The technology developed here has many space and commercial applications, including patient health monitoring, and aircraft and automotive diagnosis.

7. Potential Commercial Application(s)(Limit 200 words)

The technology developed here can be applied to real-time health monitoring of safety critical systems, such as commercial airliners, nuclear power plants, and telecommunication networks. However, few consumer applications can afford the expense of onboard diagnosis. Many incorporate sensors to monitor performance, and can be equipped with a communication link at a relatively low cost. The remarkable aspect of the technology proposed here is that it is accessible over standard cellular networks. This makes real-time diagnosis universally accessible! For example, the driver of a stalled car can use our Remote Diagnosis Server (RDS) to get a prompt diagnosis, and AAA can dispatch roadside assistance with the exact spare part required to fix the problem. Other applications include remote health monitoring of home-care patients and battlefield soldiers, round the clock monitoring of high rise buildings and their elevators, escalators, heating and ventilation systems etc. Some day, all our appliances, from the toaster oven to the dishwasher, refrigerators and washing machines, may be networked and inter-operating utilizing technologies such as Jini. Such a connected world would open doors to a huge market for RDS, with applications in monitoring and diagnosis of millions of appliances.
