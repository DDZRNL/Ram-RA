PROJECT TITLE
Measuring Small Phase Perturbations


TECHNICAL ABSTRACT (LIMIT 200 WORDS)
Visidyne proposes to critically assess the requirements for a phase processor to detect miniscule phase bumps hidden in the beat frequency signal from a pair of interfering optical beams, e.g., used to link proof masses on free flying satellites. These phase bumps, the result of a cosmological event producing gravity waves affecting the local space time frame thereby causing small length changes or strains. These length changes maybe at the level of a few picometers or strains for a gage length of 5 x 10 E6 km of 4 x 10 E-22. The proposed effort would assess these requirements in light of the errors; random and systematic associated with doing ultra high resolution optical interferometry.


POTENTIAL COMMERCIAL APPLICATIONS
The proposed effort and technology, length measurement on the picometer scale, are way in excess of any conceivable needs presently existing in the commercial marketplace. However, some of its spin-offs in the form detectors, chip sets may find use in such fields as nanometer metrology for semiconductor lithography and high resolution phase based optical sensors.
