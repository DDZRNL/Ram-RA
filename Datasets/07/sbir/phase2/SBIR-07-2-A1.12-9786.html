TECHNICAL ABSTRACT (Limit 2000 characters, approximately = 200=20 words)
The runway safety issue has been on the Most Wanted = list of the=20 National Transportation Safety Board since the list's inception in = 1990.=20 The FAA has responded by implementing two ground surveillance = technologies=20 at major U.S. airports to reduce the risk of runway incursions. = However,=20 both technologies route information through air traffic control = (rather=20 than directly to pilots), which significantly delays safe = responses.=20 Several flight deck technologies that communicate information = directly to=20 pilots are currently in development. However, there is a need for = tools to=20 rapidly test the flight deck technologies early in the design = process and=20 measure their impact on pilot performance prior to implementation. = We=20 propose to develop two tools that can be used together or = independently to=20 evaluate performance of flight deck technologies aimed at = improving runway=20 safety. We will deliver a computational cognitive model (Adaptive = Control=20 of Thought-Runway Safety; ACT-RS) that realistically emulates = pilot=20 performance, thus reducing the need for human pilots early in the = design=20 process. In addition, we will deliver a measurement tool = (Performance=20 Measurement (PM) Engine) that can measure the impact of the flight = deck=20 technology on the performance of ACT-RS and a human pilot, making = it=20 useful across the technology lifecycle.=20

POTENTIAL NASA COMMERCIAL APPLICATIONS (Limit 1500 = characters,=20 approximately 150 words)
ACT-RS and the PM Engine will be = useful to=20 NASA researchers within the Aviation Safety Program and the = Airspace=20 Systems Program as tools that will allow them to: (1) assess the = impact of=20 flight deck technologies aimed at improving runway safety = throughout the=20 design lifecycle, (2) identify the underlying factors driving=20 experience-based effects of technology implementation on pilot=20 performance, and (3) provide flexible modeling and software = frameworks for=20 assessing performance in different conditions and domains.=20
