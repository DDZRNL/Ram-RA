TECHNICAL ABSTRACT (Limit 2000 characters, approximately = 200=20 words)
Aero-assist technologies are used to control the = velocity of=20 exploration vehicles (EV) when entering earth or other planetary=20 atmospheres. Since entry of EVs in planetary atmospheres results = in=20 significant heating, thermally stable aero-assist technologies are = required to avoid the high heating rates while maintaining low = mass.=20 Polymer adhesives are used in aero-assist structures because of = the need=20 for high flexibility and good bonding between the different layers = of=20 polymer films or fabrics. However, current polymer adhesives = cannot=20 withstand temperatures above 400 C. Therefore, polymer adhesives = capable=20 of withstanding high temperatures (> 400 C) are highly = desirable for=20 NASA applications. The present proposal, a collaborative effort = between=20 NEI Corporation and a NASA supplier of inflatable structures, aims = to=20 utilize our nanotechnology capabilities to address this need. The = goal of=20 this program is to develop high temperature adhesives that exhibit = high=20 thermal conductivity in addition to increased thermal = decomposition=20 temperature. Enhanced thermal conductivity will help to dissipate = heat=20 quickly and effectively to avoid temperature rise to harmful = levels. This,=20 together with increased thermal decomposition temperature, will = enable the=20 adhesives to sustain transient high temperature conditions. A key=20 innovation of the program is to control the nanoparticle = morphology so=20 that enhanced thermal conductivity can be realized at relatively = low=20 nanoparticle loading levels. Building upon the Phase I proof of = concept,=20 the Phase II program will further develop the technology and = implement it=20 in NASA and commercial prototypes, thereby increasing the TRL to 4 = or=20 greater.=20
