TECHNICAL ABSTRACT (Limit 2000 characters, approximately = 200=20 words)
The objective of the Phase II STTR project is to = develop a=20 prototype multi-analyte sensor system to detect gaseous analytes = present=20 in the test stands during propulsion test activities. Data on the=20 concentrations of propellant-derived species are important to = assure=20 proper integration of safety protocols with testing activities to = comply=20 with environmental regulations. InnoSense LLC (ISL) has utilized = its=20 Chemical Fingerprint(TM) sensor array fabrication technology in = Phase I to=20 establish the feasibility of the multi-analyte approach. In = particular, we=20 have detected carbon dioxide, isopropanol, carbon monoxide, = kerosene and=20 ethylene as potential target analytes in the test stands. We have = employed=20 pin printing-based fabrication strategies, which would allow us to = customize the sensor suite specific to engine types using = different=20 propellants. Concerning the placement of the sensors at the test = stands,=20 NASA users will use their discretion in deciding the location = based on CFD=20 (Computational Fluid Dynamics) reports. The sensors developed = under this=20 project can be used to validate these CFD models. ISL has received = endorsement letters on the technology from two NASA prime = contractors. ISL=20 has also secured Phase III funding commitment from two = commercialization=20 partners. For assuring success of this project, ISL has assembled = a=20 technical team with a cumulative 90 person-years of experience in=20 developing commercially viable sensor systems.=20

POTENTIAL NASA COMMERCIAL APPLICATIONS (Limit 1500 = characters,=20 approximately 150 words)
This technology will help NASA = programs with=20 advanced test platforms with enhanced ability to provide = supporting=20 information for real time decision making. This sensor system will = be=20 capable of determining gas species associated with the testing of = rocket=20 engines using hydrogen, oxygen, RP1, and hybrid fuels. The = proposed system=20 can also be configured to detect, locate, and quantify leakage of = rocket=20 fuels for applications in ground testing of rocket engines, space = shuttle,=20 and next generation space vehicles. Since, there is a significant = safety=20 need to monitor the concentration of rocket fuels near launch = vehicle main=20 engines and propellant tanks, this system could be applied to = various=20 areas of concern including the engines and other subsystems during = checkout and ground operations.=20
